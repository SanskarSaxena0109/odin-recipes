Paneer Tikka

Back to Home

Paneer Tikka is a popular Indian appetizer made from paneer (Indian cottage cheese) marinated in a blend of yogurt and spices, then grilled or baked until it is charred and flavorful. The marinade typically includes ingredients like yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and lemon juice. The marinated paneer cubes are often skewered with vegetables such as bell peppers and onions before being cooked. Paneer Tikka is known for its smoky flavor and is commonly served with mint chutney or a side salad. It is a favorite dish for vegetarians and is enjoyed at parties, restaurants, and street food stalls across India.

Ingredients

For the Marinade:
  • Paneer (Indian Cottage Cheese): 250g, cut into cubes
  • Yogurt: ½ cup
  • Ginger-Garlic Paste: 1 tablespoon
  • Red Chili Powder: 1 teaspoon
  • Turmeric Powder: ½ teaspoon
  • Garam Masala: 1 teaspoon
  • Lemon Juice: 1 tablespoon
  • Salt: to taste
For the Skewers:
  • Bell Peppers: 1-2, cut into chunks
  • Onions: 1-2, cut into chunks
  • Skewers: Wooden or metal skewers (if using wooden skewers, soak them in water for 30 minutes to prevent burning)

Instructions

Marinating the Paneer:
  1. Prepare the Marinade: In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala, lemon juice, and salt. Mix well to create a smooth marinade.
  2. Marinate the Paneer: Add the paneer cubes to the marinade and gently toss to coat them evenly. Cover the bowl and let the paneer marinate in the refrigerator for at least 30 minutes, or up to 2 hours for more flavor.
Assembling and Cooking the Paneer Tikka:
  1. Preheat the Grill or Oven: If using a grill, preheat it to medium-high heat. If using an oven, preheat it to 200°C (400°F) and line a baking sheet with aluminum foil.
  2. Skewer the Paneer and Vegetables: Thread the marinated paneer cubes onto the skewers, alternating with chunks of bell peppers and onions.
  3. Grill or Bake: If grilling, place the skewers on the grill and cook for 10-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the paneer is charred and cooked through. If baking, place the skewers on the prepared baking sheet and bake for 15-20 minutes, turning halfway through, until the paneer is golden and slightly charred.
  4. Serve: Remove the paneer tikka from the grill or oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Serve hot with mint chutney or a side salad. Enjoy your delicious paneer tikka!
