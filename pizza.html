Pizza

Pizza is a beloved dish that originated in Italy and has become popular worldwide. It typically consists of a round, flat base of dough topped with tomato sauce, cheese, and various toppings such as vegetables, meats, and herbs. The pizza is then baked in an oven until the crust is crispy and the cheese is melted and bubbly. There are countless variations of pizza, from classic Margherita to creative combinations like BBQ chicken or veggie supreme. Whether you prefer thin crust or deep-dish, pizza is a versatile and delicious meal that can be enjoyed by people of all ages.

Ingredients

For the Dough:
  • Warm Water: 1 cup (approx. 110°F/43°C)
  • Instant Dry Yeast: 1.5 to 2.25 teaspoons (1 packet)
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon to 1 tablespoon (to activate yeast)
  • All-Purpose Flour or Bread Flour: 2½ to 3½ cups
  • Salt: 1 teaspoon
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons, plus more for greasing the bowl
  • Optional: Cornmeal (for dusting the pan)
For the Sauce:
  • Tomatoes: 400g-500g canned tomato sauce, puree, or crushed tomatoes
  • Garlic: 2-3 cloves, minced
  • Olive Oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Italian Seasoning: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt and Pepper: to taste
For the Toppings:
  • Shredded Mozzarella Cheese: 2 cups
  • Optional Toppings: pepperoni, mushrooms, bell peppers, onions, olives, sausage, etc.

Instructions

Making the Dough:
  1. Activate Yeast: In a large bowl, combine warm water, sugar, and yeast. Stir gently and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until it becomes frothy and bubbly.
  2. Mix: Add the flour, salt, and olive oil to the yeast mixture. Stir until a sticky dough forms
  3. Knead: Turn the dough onto a floured surface. Knead for 5–10 minutes by pushing and folding until it is smooth, elastic, and no longer sticky.
  4. First Rise: Place the dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover it with a clean kitchen towel or plastic wrap, and let it rise in a warm place for 1–2 hours until it doubles in size.
Making the Sauce:
  1. Sauté: Heat olive oil in a pan and sauté minced garlic until fragrant (about 1 minute).
  2. Simmer: Add tomato puree, salt, and sugar. Simmer on low heat for 15–20 minutes until the sauce thickens to a spreading consistency.
  3. Season: Stir in Italian herbs and black pepper, then let the sauce cool completely.
Assembling and Baking the Pizza:
  1. Preheat: Set your oven to its highest setting (usually 230–250°C / 450–500°F) for at least 20 minutes
  2. Shape the Dough: Punch down the risen dough and turn it onto a floured surface. Roll it out into a circle or rectangle, about ¼ inch thick.
  3. Prepare the Pan: If using a pizza stone, place it in the oven to preheat. If using a baking sheet, lightly grease it with olive oil and sprinkle with cornmeal to prevent sticking.
  4. Assemble: Transfer the rolled-out dough to the prepared pan. Spread a thin layer of sauce over the dough, leaving a small border around the edges. Sprinkle shredded mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce, then add your desired toppings.
  5. Bake: Place the pizza in the preheated oven and bake for 10–15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbly and slightly browned.
  6. Serve: Remove the pizza from the oven, let it cool for a few minutes, then slice and serve hot. Enjoy your homemade pizza!
